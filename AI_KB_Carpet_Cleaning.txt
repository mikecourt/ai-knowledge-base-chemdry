KEY DIFFERENTIATORS FROM STEAM CLEANING:
- Drying Time: 1-2 hours (vs. 1-2 days for steam cleaning)
- Water Usage: Approximately 80% less water than typical steam cleaning
- No Residue: No dirt-attracting soap residue left behind
- Deeper Clean: Carbonation lifts dirt to surface for extraction
- Healthier: 98% allergen removal (independently tested)

2. HOT CARBONATING EXTRACTION (HCE) SCIENCE
===========================================

HOW HCE WORKS:
Hot Carbonating Extraction is the proprietary cleaning method that sets Chem-Dry apart from traditional steam cleaning and other carpet cleaning methods.

THE 3-STEP HCE PROCESS:

STEP 1: CARBONATION APPLICATION
- The Natural¬Æ is applied to carpets
- Immediately releases millions of tiny carbonating bubbles
- Bubbles penetrate deep into carpet fibers
- Reaches down to the carpet backing

STEP 2: DIRT LIFTING
- Carbonating bubbles explode upon contact with dirt and grime
- As bubbles rise to surface, they lift particles with them
- Unlike steam cleaning which pushes dirt deeper
- Carbonation provides mechanical cleaning action

STEP 3: EXTRACTION
- Powerful vacuum system removes dirt, grime, and cleaning solution
- Simultaneous application and extraction
- Leaves carpets approximately 80% drier than steam cleaning
- No sticky residue left behind

THE NATURAL¬Æ CORE CLEANER:
The Natural¬Æ is Chem-Dry's proprietary cleaning solution.

KEY PROPERTIES:
- Green Certified by the FDA
- Non-toxic and safe for children and pets
- Contains no soaps, detergents, solvents, enzymes, or harsh chemicals
- pH level: 8.5-9.0 (slightly alkaline)
- Biodegradable formula
- VOC compliant
- Leaves no dirt-attracting residue

CARBONATION VS. STEAM CLEANING:

WATER USAGE:
- Steam Cleaning: Floods carpets with gallons of water + soap
- Chem-Dry HCE: Uses approximately 80% less water
- Result: Faster drying, less disruption, reduced mold/mildew risk

CLEANING POWER:
- Steam Cleaning: Water and soap pushed into fibers, often driving dirt deeper
- Chem-Dry HCE: Bubbles lift dirt to surface for extraction
- Result: Deeper, more effective clean

RESIDUE:
- Steam Cleaning: Soap residue acts like dirt magnet
- Chem-Dry HCE: No soap = no residue
- Result: Carpets stay clean 50% longer

HEALTH BENEFITS:
- Steam Cleaning: Excess water creates mold/mildew risk, soap chemicals affect air quality
- Chem-Dry HCE: Minimal water, no harsh chemicals, superior allergen removal
- Result: Healthier home environment

SCIENTIFIC VALIDATION:
- Independent laboratory testing shows 98% removal of common household allergens
- When combined with sanitizer: 89% removal of airborne bacteria
- Studies confirm superior cleaning performance vs. steam cleaning

WHY CARBONATION OUTPERFORMS TRADITIONAL METHODS:
1. Mechanical Action: Bubbles provide agitation that water alone cannot
2. Deep Penetration: Carbonation reaches base of carpet pile
3. Lifting Power: Rising bubbles carry dirt to surface
4. No Soap Chemistry: Avoids residue that attracts dirt
5. Minimal Moisture: Protects carpet backing and padding

4. SERVICE PROCESS (STEP-BY-STEP)
===========================================

PRE-SERVICE PREPARATION (Customer Responsibilities):
Before technician arrives, customers should:
- Remove small items, breakables, valuables from areas to be cleaned
- Vacuum high-traffic areas (light vacuuming service available for fee)
- Secure pets in separate room or outside
- Clear access paths to all areas
- Identify any special concerns or problem areas

ARRIVAL AND INTRODUCTION (5-10 minutes):
1. Arrive within scheduled time window
2. Park in designated area (driveway, not street)
3. Professional introduction with ID and business card
4. Put on shoe covers before entering
5. Quick walkthrough to confirm areas to be cleaned
6. Estimate total service time
7. Answer any immediate questions

PRE-CLEANING INSPECTION (10-15 minutes):
Critical step for managing expectations and identifying issues.

ROOM-BY-ROOM ASSESSMENT:
For each area, document:
- Overall carpet condition (excellent, good, fair, poor)
- Soil level (light, moderate, heavy)
- Stain identification (location, type, age)
- Damage assessment (wear, burns, snags, seams)
- Furniture placement and moving needs
- Special concerns (odors, high-value items nearby)

CARPET ANALYSIS:
- Fiber type identification (nylon, polyester, wool, etc.)
- Pile type (cut, loop, combination)
- Age and wear assessment
- Previous cleaning history
- Manufacturer stain protection status

STAIN EVALUATION:
For each visible stain:
- Document precise location
- Identify type if possible (wine, coffee, pet, unknown)
- Assess age (fresh, recent, old/set)
- Determine treatability:
  * Likely removable (fresh, water-based)
  * May improve (recent, treatable type)
  * Probably permanent (bleach, very old, dye damage)
- Take photos of concerning stains

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Pet odor areas (mark with flags if needed)
- High-value or delicate furniture nearby
- Moisture-sensitive areas (electronics, wood floors)
- Access challenges (narrow doorways, stairs)

CUSTOMER CONSULTATION (5-10 minutes):

REVIEW FINDINGS:
- Walk customer through inspection results
- Point out pre-existing stains and damage
- Explain realistic expectations for problem areas
- Identify what will clean well vs. what may not improve

SET EXPECTATIONS:
- Explain HCE process and how it works
- Confirm drying time (1-2 hours)
- Discuss traffic restrictions during drying
- Address customer questions or concerns

RECOMMEND ADDITIONAL SERVICES:
Based on findings, suggest:
- Pet treatment for odor areas
- Stain protection application
- Specialty stain removal for tough spots
- Additional rooms or areas

GET APPROVAL:
- Confirm final scope of work
- Get signature on work order
- Note any special instructions
- Confirm payment method

FURNITURE MOVING (5-15 minutes):

WHAT WE MOVE (Included):
- Light furniture under 50 lbs (one person can safely move)
- Dining chairs, end tables, ottomans
- Standard sofas and coffee tables
- Items that can be moved by one technician

WHAT WE DON'T MOVE:
- Beds and dressers
- China cabinets or heavy entertainment centers
- Pianos or organs
- Electronics (TVs, stereos, computers)
- Items over 100 lbs
- Fragile or antique furniture
- Anything with items on/in it

MOVING PROCESS:
1. Place protective blocks or tabs under furniture legs
2. Move furniture to cleaned areas when possible
3. Clean exposed areas first
4. Return furniture to original position
5. Ensure blocks/tabs remain under legs until carpet is completely dry

PRE-TREATMENT (10-20 minutes for average home):

TRAFFIC AREAS:
- Identify heavy traffic lanes and entrance areas
- Apply traffic lane pre-treatment
- Allow 3-5 minutes dwell time
- Agitate with grooming brush if needed

SPOT TREATMENT:
- Apply appropriate spot remover to identified stains
- Match treatment to stain type when possible
- Allow proper dwell time per product specifications
- Agitate gently with bone tool or brush
- Avoid over-wetting or over-application

SPECIAL TREATMENTS:
- Pet enzyme treatment for urine areas
- Specialty treatments for specific stain types
- Extra attention to problem areas identified in inspection

HOT CARBONATING EXTRACTION - MAIN CLEANING:

SETUP:
- Position equipment to minimize hose dragging
- Ensure adequate ventilation
- Set proper solution temperature (160-180¬∞F at wand)
- Mix The Natural¬Æ per specifications

CLEANING TECHNIQUE:

PATTERN:
- Work in overlapping passes
- Maintain consistent wand speed
- Forward pass: Apply solution while moving forward
- Backward pass: Extract while moving backward
- Overlap each pass by 2-3 inches
- Make two passes over each area (four total strokes)

SEQUENCE:
1. Start in farthest room, work toward exit
2. Clean along walls and edges first with detail tool
3. Clean open areas with wand in overlapping rows
4. Work backward toward doorway
5. Avoid walking on wet carpet when possible

HIGH TRAFFIC AREAS:
- Make additional passes (3-4 total)
- Use slower wand speed for maximum extraction
- Ensure thorough soil removal

CORNERS AND EDGES:
- Use detail tool for tight spaces
- Get as close to baseboards as possible
- Avoid leaving dark lines along walls

POST-CLEANING GROOMING (5-10 minutes):

PILE LIFTING:
- Use carpet grooming rake on matted traffic areas
- Lift pile in high-use zones
- Create uniform appearance

SPOT CHECK:
- Inspect for missed areas
- Do touch-ups as needed
- Re-treat any remaining spots

FINAL WALKTHROUGH (10-15 minutes):

QUALITY CHECK:
- Walk customer through each cleaned area
- Point out results, especially previously-stained areas
- Explain any spots that didn't fully remove
- Ensure customer satisfaction

POST-CARE INSTRUCTIONS (Verbal and Written):

DRYING GUIDELINES:
- Expect 1-2 hours drying time
- Keep foot traffic minimal during drying
- Use fans or AC to accelerate drying if desired
- Open windows if weather permits
- Keep pets off carpets until fully dry

FURNITURE REPLACEMENT:
- Leave blocks/tabs under furniture legs for 24 hours
- Don't place furniture back until carpet is completely dry
- If must replace sooner, use blocks/tabs to prevent rust stains

MAINTENANCE TIPS:
- Vacuum 2-3 times per week
- Address spills immediately with white towel blotting
- Avoid walking on carpets with shoes
- Schedule next cleaning in 6-12 months

PROTECTANT INFORMATION (If Applied):
- New protectant needs 24 hours to fully cure
- Avoid deep cleaning or shampooing for 30 days
- Benefits include stain resistance and easier future cleaning

PAYMENT AND PAPERWORK (5 minutes):
- Review work performed
- Confirm all areas addressed
- Process payment
- Provide receipt and invoice
- Leave care instruction sheet
- Provide contact information for questions
- Explain satisfaction guarantee
- Request review/referral
- Schedule next appointment if desired

TYPICAL SERVICE TIMING:

AVERAGE 3-BEDROOM HOME (approximately 1,200 sq ft):
- Arrival and inspection: 15-20 minutes
- Furniture moving: 10 minutes
- Pre-treatment: 15 minutes
- Cleaning: 45-60 minutes
- Post-grooming and walkthrough: 15 minutes
- TOTAL: 1.5-2 hours

LARGER HOMES OR HEAVY SOIL: Add 30-60 minutes
SMALL APARTMENTS (2 rooms): 45-60 minutes total

6. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
===========================================

ABOUT THE PROCESS:

Q: How does Chem-Dry differ from steam cleaning?
A: The biggest differences are water usage and cleaning method. Traditional steam cleaning floods carpets with gallons of water mixed with soap. The Chem-Dry process uses approximately 80% less water and relies on carbonation instead of soap. Think of it like club soda lifting a stain - millions of tiny carbonating bubbles penetrate deep into carpet fibers, lifting dirt and grime to the surface where we extract it. Because we use so much less water and no soap, carpets dry in 1-2 hours instead of 1-2 days, and there's no sticky residue left behind to attract dirt.

Q: How long will my carpets take to dry?
A: Typically 1-2 hours. Because we use approximately 80% less water than steam cleaning, you can walk on carpets and return furniture the same day. Most customers have carpets cleaned in the morning and are back to normal life by afternoon. Some factors can affect drying (humidity, ventilation, carpet thickness), but even in less-ideal conditions, carpets are usually dry within 2-4 hours maximum.

Q: Is the cleaning solution safe for children and pets?
A: Absolutely! The Natural¬Æ, our core cleaner, is so safe it's Green Certified by the FDA. It doesn't rely on harsh chemicals - it uses the power of carbonation. Many customers specifically choose us because they have young children or pets and want effective but safe cleaning. Kids and pets can be back on carpets as soon as they're dry, typically within 1-2 hours.

Q: Do you move furniture?
A: Yes, we move most standard furniture as part of service. This includes dining chairs, end tables, coffee tables, and most sofas. We place protective blocks or tabs under furniture legs to prevent rust stains or damage while carpet dries. We don't move very heavy items (beds, dressers, entertainment centers, pianos) or delicate antiques. If specific furniture concerns exist, discuss when scheduling.

Q: How often should I have my carpets cleaned?
A: Depends on household:
- Low traffic, no pets: Every 12-18 months
- Average family, moderate traffic: Every 9-12 months
- High traffic or homes with pets: Every 6-9 months
- Allergy sufferers: Every 6 months

Regular professional cleaning removes allergens, extends carpet life, and maintains a healthier home. Many customers clean less often than with traditional methods because our process doesn't leave residue that attracts dirt back quickly.

Q: Can you remove all stains?
A: We're honest - not all stains can be removed. Some stains (bleach spots, very old set-in stains) are permanent because they've actually changed the carpet fiber color. However, we have excellent success with most common stains. When we arrive, we inspect carpets and explain what we can realistically accomplish. For tough stains, specialty treatments are available. We set proper expectations, then do everything we can to exceed them. Remember: we have a satisfaction guarantee - if you're not happy, we come back and try again at no charge.

Q: Will cleaning remove pet odors?
A: Yes! We have specific enzyme treatments that break down urine crystals causing odors. This eliminates smell at source rather than just masking with fragrances. Level of odor removal depends on contamination depth. Surface-level pet accidents respond excellently. If urine has soaked into padding or subfloor, additional treatment may be recommended for complete removal. We'll assess your situation and recommend best approach.

ABOUT RESULTS:

Q: Will my carpets stay clean longer?
A: Yes! This is a key benefit. Because we don't use soap or detergents, there's no dirt-attracting residue. With traditional steam cleaning, soap residue acts like a dirt magnet, which is why carpets get dirty again quickly. Our customers consistently report carpets stay cleaner much longer - often twice as long as previous cleaning methods.

Q: Will cleaning restore carpet texture?
A: We can definitely improve matted or crushed fibers! We groom carpet after cleaning to lift pile and restore texture. However, if fibers are permanently worn or damaged from years of heavy traffic, cleaning won't reverse that wear. What we can do is remove dirt and grime making worn areas look worse, making carpet look as good as its condition allows.

Q: Why do some spots come back after cleaning?
A: If spots reappear, it's typically "wicking." This happens when contamination deep in carpet padding or backing wicks back to surface as it dries - like a candle wick drawing wax upward. This isn't a problem with our cleaning - it reveals contamination that was there all along. If this happens, call us! We'll return and treat those areas specifically. Usually a second treatment with proper drying techniques resolves the issue permanently.

10. QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS
===========================================

INITIAL CONTACT QUESTIONS:

1. "What prompted you to look into carpet cleaning today?"
   Purpose: Understand motivation and pain points
   Listen for: Specific event, problem, routine maintenance, external pressure

2. "How long has it been since your carpets were professionally cleaned?"
   Purpose: Gauge carpet condition and set expectations
   Listen for: Never/don't remember, recent, long ago

3. "What type of carpet do you have - cut pile, berber, or something else?"
   Purpose: Identify carpet type for proper treatment
   Listen for: Specific answer, "not sure," mentions expensive/brand name

4. "Tell me about any specific stains or problem areas you have."
   Purpose: Set expectations and identify specialty treatment needs
   Listen for: Type/age of stains, pet issues, heavy traffic, frustration

HOUSEHOLD COMPOSITION QUESTIONS:

5. "Do you have children, pets, or both?"
   Purpose: Identify lifestyle factors affecting carpets
   If children: Emphasize safety, allergen removal, quick drying
   If pets: Ask about accidents/odors, position pet treatment, discuss allergen removal
   If both: Strong emphasis on health/safety, quick drying critical

6. "Does anyone in your household have allergies or asthma?"
   Purpose: Position health benefits
   If yes: Lead with 98% allergen removal, emphasize no harsh chemicals

PROPERTY QUESTIONS:

7. "How many rooms are you looking to have cleaned?"
   Purpose: Gauge project size and budget
   Follow-up: "Which rooms specifically?" "Any stairs or hallways?"

8. "Are your carpets wall-to-wall or do you also have area rugs?"
   Purpose: Identify additional service opportunities
   If area rugs: Position area rug cleaning as add-on

9. "What type of subfloor do you have - concrete or wood?"
   Purpose: Identify potential issues and adjust technique
   Concrete: May have moisture/odor issues
   Wood: Emphasize low-water process, quick drying even more important

EXPERIENCE & EXPECTATIONS QUESTIONS:

10. "Have you had bad experiences with carpet cleaning before?"
    Purpose: Uncover objections before they become obstacles
    If yes: Let them explain, show empathy, explain how you're different

11. "What's most important to you in a carpet cleaning service?"
    Purpose: Understand priorities to customize pitch
    Common answers: Price ‚Üí value/savings; Results ‚Üí HCE/guarantee; Safety ‚Üí Green Certified/allergen removal; Convenience ‚Üí quick drying/flexible scheduling; Trust ‚Üí years in business/reviews

URGENCY & TIMELINE QUESTIONS:

12. "When were you hoping to have this done?"
    Purpose: Gauge urgency and create timeline
    Listen for: Specific date/event, "whenever," "ASAP"

13. "Is this for a special occasion or routine maintenance?"
    Purpose: Understand motivation level
    Special occasion: Creates urgency, more likely to commit
    Routine: May be more price-sensitive, less urgent

BUDGET & DECISION QUESTIONS:

14. "Have you gotten other quotes, or is this your first call?"
    Purpose: Understand where you are in buying process
    First call: You set expectations, educate thoroughly
    Other quotes: "What did you learn?" Differentiate on value

15. "Who else is involved in this decision?"
    Purpose: Identify decision-makers
    If spouse/partner: "Will they be present for estimate?" Address both parties' concerns

16. "What's your budget for this project?"
    Purpose: Qualify financially (use carefully - only if customer seems very price-focused)

CLOSING/COMMITMENT QUESTIONS:

17. "Based on what we've discussed, does this sound like something that would work for you?"
    Purpose: Trial close
    If yes: "Great! Let's get you scheduled."
    If hesitation: "What questions do you still have?"

18. "Which works better for you - morning or afternoon?"
    Purpose: Assumptive close (offers choice of times, not choice to book)
