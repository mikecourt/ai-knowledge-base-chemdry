ABOUT THE PROCESS:

Q: How is upholstery cleaning different from carpet cleaning?
A: While both use Hot Carbonating Extraction technology, upholstery cleaning requires specialized training and equipment adapted for furniture. Upholstered furniture has different fabrics, construction methods, and cleaning codes than carpet. We use specialized tools designed for furniture contours, seams, and delicate fabrics.

Q: How long does upholstery cleaning take?
A: For a typical three-piece set (sofa, loveseat, chair), service usually takes 45-90 minutes. A single sofa typically takes 20-30 minutes. Exact time depends on size, soil level, and whether adding protectant or specialty treatments.

Q: How long until my furniture is dry?
A: Most upholstered furniture dries in 1-2 hours, thanks to our low-moisture process. We use approximately 80% less water than steam cleaning, which would leave furniture wet for 12-24 hours. Drying varies by fabric type, humidity, and ventilation.

Q: Is your cleaning safe for all types of upholstery?
A: We can clean most "W" (water-safe) and "WS" (water or solvent) coded fabrics. Before starting, our technician checks the cleaning code tag on your furniture (under cushions or on bottom). We cannot clean "S" (solvent only) or "X" (vacuum only) fabrics. We assess condition and inform you of any concerns before proceeding.

Q: Will cleaning remove pet hair?
A: We remove loose pet hair during pre-vacuuming, but our process focuses on soil, stains, and odors. For best results, vacuum furniture thoroughly before our arrival, or add our pre-vacuuming service for additional fee. For deeply embedded hair, consider lint roller as ongoing maintenance.

Q: Can you remove all stains from my furniture?
A: Not all stains can be completely removed. Some stains, especially old ones or those treated incorrectly at home, may have permanently bonded with fabric fibers. However, we have excellent success with most common stains (food, beverages, body oils). We assess each stain and provide realistic expectations. Specialty stain treatments available for tough stains.

Q: What about pet odors in furniture?
A: For pet odors, we have specialized P.U.R.T.¬Æ (Pet Urine Removal Treatment) that uses enzyme action to destroy urine crystals at molecular level. Standard cleaning helps surface odors, but P.U.R.T.¬Æ is needed for urine soaked into cushions or padding. This permanently eliminates odors rather than masking them.
