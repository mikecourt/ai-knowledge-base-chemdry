2. THE SCIENCE BEHIND DRYER VENT CLEANING
========================================

HOW LINT ACCUMULATES
--------------------
Even with regular lint trap cleaning, approximately 50% of lint escapes into the vent system during the drying cycle. This lint is carried by hot, moist air through the ductwork toward the exterior vent.

As air cools and slows down (especially in longer vents or those with multiple bends), lint begins to accumulate on duct walls. Fabric softener and dryer sheets create a waxy residue that causes lint to stick more aggressively to duct surfaces.

Over time, this buildup restricts airflow, forcing the dryer to work harder and run longer.

THE FIRE TRIANGLE
------------------
Dryer fires occur when three elements combine:

1. FUEL (LINT)
   - Lint is extremely flammable
   - Accumulates throughout vent system
   - Ignition point: approximately 390-420Â°F

2. HEAT
   - Dryers generate 120-160Â°F during normal operation
   - Heating elements reach much higher temperatures
   - Restricted vents cause dangerous heat buildup

3. OXYGEN
   - Restricted airflow causes heat buildup
   - Still provides enough oxygen for combustion
   - If temperatures reach lint's ignition point, fire can start

CRITICAL SAFETY NOTE: Fires can start while the dryer is running OR shortly after it's turned off when heat cannot dissipate properly.

AIRFLOW AND EFFICIENCY
-----------------------
Dryers rely on continuous airflow to remove moisture from clothes.

When vents are clogged:
â€¢ Moisture backs up into dryer drum, causing longer drying times
â€¢ Dryer overheating triggers thermal fuses and safety switches
â€¢ Energy consumption increases dramatically
â€¢ Mechanical wear accelerates on motor, heating element, components

Clean vent system maintains optimal airflow (1,500-2,500 cubic feet per minute), allowing moisture to exhaust efficiently and keeping dryer within normal temperature ranges.
