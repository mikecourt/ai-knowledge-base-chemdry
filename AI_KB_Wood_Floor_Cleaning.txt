5. FLOOR FINISH IDENTIFICATION
================================================================================

COMMON FLOOR FINISHES:

Polyurethane (Most Common):
‚Ä¢ Hard, protective coating
‚Ä¢ Water-based or oil-based
‚Ä¢ Can be cleaned with our process
‚Ä¢ Glossy, semi-gloss, or satin appearance

Varnish:
‚Ä¢ Traditional finish, less common now
‚Ä¢ Similar to polyurethane
‚Ä¢ Can be cleaned with our process

Wax:
‚Ä¢ Soft finish that requires buffing
‚Ä¢ Not suitable for our cleaning process
‚Ä¢ Needs specialized wax maintenance
‚Ä¢ Rare in modern homes

Penetrating Oil:
‚Ä¢ Soaks into wood rather than coating
‚Ä¢ Requires different maintenance
‚Ä¢ May not be suitable for our process
‚Ä¢ Less common

TESTING UNKNOWN FINISHES:

Water Drop Test:
1. Place small water drop on inconspicuous area
2. If water beads up: Likely poly or varnish (safe to clean)
3. If water soaks in: Likely wax or oil finish (caution needed)

Scratch Test:
1. Use fingernail on inconspicuous area
2. If you can scratch surface easily: Likely wax
3. If surface is hard: Likely poly or varnish

When in Doubt:
‚Ä¢ Test in small, hidden area
‚Ä¢ Start with most gentle approach
‚Ä¢ Watch for any adverse reactions
‚Ä¢ Consult manufacturer information if available

7. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

ABOUT THE PROCESS:

Q: How is your wood floor cleaning different from mopping?
A: Great question! Traditional mopping actually has several problems: it spreads
dirty water around rather than removing soil, leaves residue that dulls your
floors, uses too much water that can seep between boards, and the results only
last a few days.

Our professional cleaning uses minimal moisture with wood-safe, pH-balanced
solutions specifically designed for hardwood floors. We actually remove soil and
buildup rather than spreading it around. Your floors dry in 30-60 minutes
instead of staying wet for hours, there's no dulling residue, and results last
12-18 months. Plus, we do all the work!

Q: Will this damage my hardwood floors?
A: Absolutely not - in fact, we protect them! Before we start, we verify your
floor finish type to ensure compatibility. Our process uses minimal moisture
(floors dry in 30-60 minutes), pH-balanced solutions that won't harm finishes,
and soft tools that protect your floors.

What DOES damage floors is improper cleaning - using too much water, harsh
chemicals like vinegar or ammonia, or abrasive tools. We avoid all of those.
Our process is specifically designed to clean and protect hardwood floors.

Q: How long does the service take?
A: It depends on square footage. A typical room (200 sq ft) takes about 45-60
minutes. An average whole-house service (1,000-1,500 sq ft) takes 2-3 hours.
Your floors will be ready to walk on in clean socks immediately after we finish,
and fully dry in 30-60 minutes.

Q: Do I need to move my furniture?
A: No - we clean around furniture. We don't move heavy pieces like sofas, beds,
or entertainment centers. We can clean under furniture if it's accessible
without moving (like under a sofa on legs). You should remove small items, area
rugs, and floor clutter before we arrive, but the furniture stays in place.

Q: How often should I have my hardwood floors professionally cleaned?
A: We recommend every 12-18 months for most homes. However, you might clean
more frequently if you have:
‚Ä¢ High traffic areas or large families: Every 6-12 months
‚Ä¢ Commercial spaces: Every 3-6 months
‚Ä¢ Mostly low traffic: Every 18-24 months

Regular professional cleaning extends your floors' life, maintains their beauty,
and delays the need for expensive refinishing.

ABOUT RESULTS:

Q: Will cleaning make my dull floors shiny again?
A: In most cases, yes! Dull floors are usually caused by buildup from cleaning
products, body oils, cooking oils, and general soil. Our cleaning removes this
buildup, revealing the natural shine of your finish underneath.

However, if your floor finish is worn through or severely damaged, cleaning
will improve appearance but can't restore a worn finish. In those cases, we can
clean the floors and recommend finish renewal treatment, or suggest refinishing
if the finish is too worn.

Q: Can you remove scratches from my floors?
A: Cleaning removes dirt from scratches, which often makes them less noticeable.
However, we can't remove deep scratches that are actually in the wood or finish
- those would require buffing or refinishing. We can discuss spot repair options
for minor scratches, or recommend a refinishing professional for severe damage.

Q: Will my floors look brand new?
A: Your floors will look significantly better - brighter, cleaner, and shinier.
If the finish is in good condition, they can look close to new! However, we
want to set realistic expectations: cleaning reveals what's there. If floors
have wear patterns, deep scratches, or finish damage, cleaning will improve
appearance but can't reverse physical wear. For floors to truly look new, they'd
need complete refinishing.

ABOUT SAFETY AND HEALTH:

Q: Are your cleaning solutions safe for my family and pets?
A: Yes! Our wood floor cleaning solutions are pH-balanced and specifically
formulated to be safe for families and pets. We don't use harsh chemicals,
ammonia, or toxic substances. Your family and pets can return to the floors as
soon as they're dry - typically 30-60 minutes.

Q: Is there a strong odor during or after cleaning?
A: No. Our wood floor cleaning solutions have minimal odor - nothing like the
strong chemical smells of some cleaners or the intense fumes from floor
refinishing. You might notice a light, clean scent during the service, but it
dissipates quickly.

Q: Will cleaning help with allergies?
A: Yes, it can help! While hardwood floors don't trap allergens like carpet
does, they still accumulate dust, pollen, pet dander, and other allergens on
the surface. Our thorough cleaning removes these allergens, which can improve
indoor air quality and help allergy sufferers.
